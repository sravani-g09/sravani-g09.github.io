Case 01 · E-Commerce Operations

Re-platforming a storefront without losing a sale

Role: Project Lead Company: Electric Bike Company Timeline: 6 months · ahead of deadline

A full migration from WooCommerce to Shopify — moving the entire catalog, customer base, and order history while the store kept selling. The goal wasn't a redesign; it was a faster, cheaper, more scalable operation that the numbers could prove.

Context

The challenge

The existing WooCommerce storefront had become the bottleneck. Pages loaded in 5.8 seconds, 72% of carts were abandoned, and order fulfillment and inventory were handled through manual, error-prone steps. The platform couldn't keep pace with growth, and it definitely couldn't support the planned international expansion.

The constraint that shaped everything: this is a live, revenue-generating store. The migration had to happen without dropping a single live sale — no data loss across the catalog, customers, or order history, and no broken checkout during cutover.

Approach

How I ran it

I phased the work so risk stayed low and the live store was never exposed. Each phase had a clear exit before the next began.

1

Audit & clean

Inventoried 520 SKUs and 18.4K customer records, deduplicated and standardized data before anything moved — migration quality is set here, not at cutover.

2

Rebuild & integrate

Built Shopify backend workflows for inventory sync, fulfillment, and service, then re-integrated 14 third-party apps so no capability was lost in the move.

3

Redesign the journey

Reworked UI/UX to attack the 72% abandonment rate directly — fewer steps, faster pages, clearer path to checkout.

4

Validate & cut over

Measured every KPI against the WooCommerce baseline before go-live, then trained marketing, support, and operations on the new system.

The analysis

Where the savings actually came from

Headline percentages are easy to wave around. Here's the operational math that makes the cost savings real — the part I care about most as an operations lead.

Labor saved from faster order processing
5 min saved/order × 2,400 orders/mo = 12,000 min/mo
12,000 min ÷ 60 = 200 labor hours/mo
200 hrs × $22/hr = $4,400/mo
+ Stockout revenue recovered from 99.2% inventory accuracy = $6,200/mo
≈ $10,600/mo recovered · ~$127K annualized

Reflection

What I'd carry forward

  • Data hygiene before cutover is the whole game. The clean-up phase felt slow, but it's why 26K orders and 18.4K customers migrated without corruption.
  • Tie every technical change to an operational number. "Faster site" doesn't land with leadership; "$4,400/month in recovered labor" does.
  • Train the humans, not just the system. Adoption across marketing, support, and ops is what made the new platform stick after go-live.
