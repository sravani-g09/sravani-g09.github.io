Case 03 · Enterprise IT

Holding the line on reliability and cost

Role: IT Project Coordinator Company: IBM & Kyndryl Clients: Global enterprise (aerospace, financial services)

Infrastructure projects for global enterprise clients leave no room for surprises. Run on disciplined RAID tracking and tight vendor negotiation, these environments held 99.98% uptime while procurement costs came down.

Context

The challenge

Enterprise clients (Bombardier, Hartford) required high-availability infrastructure under strict SLAs — uptime wasn't a target, it was a contractual obligation. At the same time, leadership wanted procurement costs reduced without any drop in service quality. Two pressures that usually pull against each other.

Bombardier · aerospace
Hartford · financial services
Approach

How I ran it

Reliability came from disciplined risk and issue management; savings came from proactive vendor work. I kept both moving in parallel with tight cross-team follow-through so nothing slipped between functions.

  • Tracked milestones and managed RAID logs across all client environments to keep risks visible and owned
  • Facilitated cross-functional meetings, maintained action-item logs, and drove issues to closure to protect SLAs
  • Negotiated vendor contracts and IT procurement, expanding B2B relationships while cutting cost
  • Mentored new hires across 5 cross-functional teams to keep delivery capacity stable
Results

Outcome

99.98%
system uptime across all environments
15%
annual cost savings, exceeding ROI targets
−18%
team turnover through structured engagement
Reflection

What I'd carry forward

  • A RAID log only works if issues actually close. The discipline was in the follow-through, not the tracking.
  • Cost and reliability aren't a trade-off if you negotiate well. Better vendor terms funded stability rather than competing with it.
