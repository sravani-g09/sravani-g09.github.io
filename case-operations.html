Case 02 · Operations

Consolidating four departments into one system

Role: IT Operations Project Lead Company: Electric Bike Company Team: 50+ staff, incl. 5 remote specialists

Finance, HR, IT, and Operations were each running on their own contracts, manual payroll steps, and disconnected tools. I rebuilt them into standardized, centrally governed workflows — and leadership got a single source of truth.

Context

The challenge

Four departments, four ways of working. Vendor contracts overlapped, payroll involved manual handoffs, and each function used its own platforms. The result was duplicated effort, unclear ownership, and no consolidated view for the CEO to make decisions from.

Approach

How I ran it

I used Lean and ITIL to map current-state workflows across all four functions, find the redundant dependencies, and design standardized processes that could be governed centrally instead of department by department. Documentation and dashboards came next, so the new way of working was visible and durable.

  • Consolidated vendor contracts, payroll operations, and digital platforms across Finance, HR, IT, and Operations
  • Stood up a centralized documentation repository — requirements, RAID logs, change requests, SOPs — across SharePoint and Confluence
  • Ran concurrent delivery through Jira, Asana, and Monday.com, tracking milestones and dependencies
  • Built Power BI and Salesforce dashboards surfacing KPIs, risks, and gaps for senior leadership

Reflection

What I'd carry forward

  • Standardize before you automate. Mapping the real workflow first is what made consolidation stick rather than just moving the mess to new tools.
  • Dashboards change behavior. Once leadership could see KPIs and risks in one place, decisions sped up on their own.
